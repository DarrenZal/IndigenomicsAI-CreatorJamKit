# Compositional Field Orientation — Plain-Language Summary

## What is the compositional field?

The compositional field is the architecture the Jam sits inside. It is not a product, a platform, or a chatbot. It is a way of describing the **shared underlying shape** that several adjacent projects have all been building toward from different angles — Indigenomics AI, SalishOakSeeds Living Atlas, Salish Sea Dreaming, Regen AI + KOI, Spore, Intelligence Commons, Poietic Match, and the bioregional Herring Pool work.

Stated as one sentence: **a compositional field architecture is infrastructure for the stewardship of witnessed commitments, across past evidence, present capacity, and future pledge, held together by cultivation rather than command.**

You do not need to memorise that sentence. You do need to know what it asks of a Jam team.

## The five-layer stack

Every project inside the compositional field uses the same five layers, with different content:

1. **Evidence** — what is known. Assertions, measurements, oral testimony, records, semantic search over a knowledge graph.
2. **Commitment** — what is promised or certified. Intents, offers, needs, promises, attestations of past action.
3. **Gap** — what is missing. Where evidence does not yet cover commitment; where commitments do not yet cover need.
4. **Consequence** — what follows when evidence meets commitment. Settlement, recognition, governance actions.
5. **Learning** — how pattern accumulates across cycles so subsequent care is wiser than earlier care.

The gap is a **first-class object**, not a derived metric. Carol Anne's framing at the April 17 Victoria demo — *"the bottom-up would pass the top-down"* — is a statement about the gap layer. Flow-first accounting (commitments, contributions, restoration labour) accumulates a record the stock-first lens of GDP / IPEA was never trying to see.

## Six load-bearing ideas

The compositional-field synthesis names eight governing principles. Six matter most for Jam teams:

### Composable stewardship

The capacity-name for what the architecture supports: **the enactment of care at scale, across sovereignty boundaries and generations, without enclosure.** "Stewardship" is what happens (care, repair, witness). "Composable" is what makes it scale (the same primitives compose across kin-group, Nation, bioregion, and pan-bioregional registers). The work the Jam does is one instance of composable stewardship; it is not the only one, and it is not the most authoritative.

### Wise legibility (from Ruddick)

> *"What communities need is not perfect visibility, but wise legibility. Enough visibility for stewardship. Enough privacy for dignity. Enough accountability for repair. Enough local judgment for the record to remain human."*

Translated for a spec: **not everything should be rendered.** Some things should remain locally held. Some should remain contestable. Some should remain partial. A spec that maximises transparency is making a category error; the question is always *which legibility, for whom, to what end, at whose authority?*

### Marker-only boundaries

Some contributions are public-readable; some are restricted to specific roles or jurisdictions; some are intentionally not rendered in any machine-legible form. The Jam's offering specs use **marker-only boundaries** — the system can see that a boundary exists (so it routes around) but cannot see the content beyond. This is how a public agent can compose with private contributions without violating their privacy.

### Dark matter legibility (from Johar)

The substrate of obligation and coupling through which value actually flows is largely invisible in standard accounting. Johar calls it *dark matter*. Governance at bioregional and longer scale requires rendering some of this dark matter legible (supply chains, reciprocity flows, infrastructural interdependencies) so it becomes governable. This composes with wise legibility: render some, hold some wise, leave some unrendered. Hold all three at once.

### Untracked allocation

Every commitment pool reserves an explicitly **non-graph-legible portion** (roughly 15–25%) for stewardship work that does not compress into assertions: slow relational repair, ceremonial knowledge, care that cannot be rendered. The fraction is visible; the contents are opaque. The principle: *some part of stewardship must remain allocable without graph proof, or the graph will eventually colonise the field it claims to serve.*

### Infinite game over finite game (from Carse, via the Ethereum Foundation)

> *"A finite game is played for the purpose of winning. An infinite game for the purpose of continuing the play."*

The Jam optimises for **continuing the play** — for keeping the relationships, the witnessing capacity, and the field alive — not for delivering a finished thing. A spec that wins the weekend by burning down the conditions for next weekend has failed the infinite-game test, no matter how impressive its output looks.

## The dream-to-fulfillment spectrum

The deepest primitive: a single morphological arc from *regenerative desire* to *witnessed fulfillment*:

> **dream → vision → care → offer / need → promise → witnessed commitment → fulfilled-and-witnessed**

Three properties matter:

1. **Gradient, not binary.** Anyone can place themselves anywhere on the spectrum. "Expression of care" is first-class; no one is required to commit. Workshops that demand explicit commitment from everyone coerce by default. A spectrum UI invites participation at the intensity the person is ready for.
2. **Giving-oriented.** Commitments describe what is offered or what was given and witnessed. The complementary dual — needs and wants — gets equal witnessing weight. Mapping the gap between offered and needed is what makes Carol Anne's *"bottom-up passes top-down"* concrete.
3. **Past / present / future are distinct.** Past = certifications of what was given. Future = promissory commitments. Present = capacity (a field-state, not a commitment). Collapsing the three into one timeline produces fuzzy results.

## Gardens, not engines

The architecture earns the metaphor **garden** — not engine, not platform, not factory. Gardens are cultivated, reproductive, diverse, place-specific, seasonal, tended. Gardens have garden-keeper roles (seed-holder, soil-keeper, weaver, harvester, witness), seasonal cycles with ceremonial transitions, anti-patterns they have to be defended against, and a default toward reproducing the conditions that let the garden continue.

Four anti-patterns sharpen the distinction:

- **Not a farm.** Single-crop, yield-optimised, place-indifferent. A farm logic maximises extractable signal per unit of witness.
- **Not a wilderness.** Un-stewarded. Anyone contributes anything; gets captured by loudest voices.
- **Not a museum.** Fixed forms, no reproduction. Freezes the corpus as canonical.
- **Not a product.** Owned, branded, controlled, eventually sold. Category error about what is being made.

The reproductive substance underneath the garden is what Ruddick names the **commons of care** — *"the most foundational, highest-prevalence form of commoning, underwriting the viability of every other commons by enabling trust, coordination, and rule-maintenance."* Carol Anne's *"wellness is the new GDP"* lands here with an economic-substantive counterpart: the wellness GDP of a commons of care is the reproductive labour that holds the field together, and an architecture that does not make it visible is one that continues to naturalise it as background.

## What this asks of a Jam team's spec

- **Name the gap your spec is for.** Not just the feature — the gap between what is being measured and what is actually happening. If the spec does not surface a gap, it is probably reproducing the dominant frame.
- **Hold marker-only boundaries.** Some contributions cannot be public. Your spec should describe the boundary, not the content beyond it.
- **Default to wise legibility.** Render less than you can. Ask, for every field: *who needs to see this, and what does seeing it cost the person whose record it is?*
- **Reserve untracked allocation.** Whatever percentage of the work happens off-graph (relational repair, deliberation, ceremonial timing), name it. Do not pretend the graph is the whole story.
- **Build for the infinite game.** What you ship at the Jam should make the next Jam more possible, not less. The relationships are the product; the artefact is the residue.
- **Stay close to the garden metaphor.** If a spec implies a factory, a marketplace, a chatbot, or an audit system, the metaphor is wrong even if the words are right. Cultivate.
