# Commitment Pool Dream Witness Composition

This directory contains a fixture-backed worked composition reference for:

`dream / offer / promise / refusal -> commitment pool -> reviewer witness receipt`

All records are fictional public-sample data. The experiment tests whether a dream-to-fulfillment board can compose with a commitment-pool route diagnostic without pressuring dreams into commitments, routing around refusals, or treating review/witness receipts as cultural or ceremonial authority.

## Question

Can a commitment pool accept candidate commitments derived from dreams, offers, and promises while preserving contributor authority, time windows, pool capacity, refusal boundaries, withdrawal paths, and AI-use receipts?

## Finding

Yes, for this fictional fixture, but only through explicit authority and transition records.

One contributor-authorized dream becomes a small time-bounded commitment candidate. A facilitator records the contributor's authorization but does not create the commitment on the contributor's behalf. A near-full pool accepts the small commitment and returns a larger candidate because capacity is insufficient. A protected source remains excluded. An already-accepted commitment includes a post-acceptance withdrawal path that adjusts capacity and downstream expectations.

## Participant Safety

- This experiment uses fictional public-sample data only.
- No participant-private, protected, cultural, ceremonial, linguistic, Nation-specific, credential-bearing, or authority-bound content is included.
- A marker-only source record demonstrates a non-use boundary. It is excluded from the composed output and no underlying content is described, summarized, inferred, routed, indexed, trained on, or displayed.
- Witness records here are software/reviewer receipts only. They are not ceremonial witnessing, Knowledge Keeper witnessing, cultural authority, or protocol authority.

## Composition Boundaries

- A dream can remain a dream.
- A dream can become a commitment only when the original contributor authorizes that exact transition.
- A facilitator can record witnessed authorization, but cannot author the transition without contributor consent.
- A pool capacity check can return a candidate without deleting or devaluing the record.
- Post-acceptance withdrawal is different from withdrawing a dream. It can affect pool capacity, witness receipts, and any downstream flow-funding edge.
- AI-use receipts are present on all transition records, even where `ai_used: false`.
- Composed outputs include `derived_from_transitions`.

## Not Established

This worked composition reference does not establish:

- real-world commitment
- funding eligibility
- authority
- legitimacy
- certification
- cultural fit
- ceremonial witnessing
- permission for other uses

## Not Run

- No live pool update.
- No real commitment, money, grant, or allocation routing.
- No participant or community review workflow.
- No public receipt wall export.
- No AI processing of private or protected material.
