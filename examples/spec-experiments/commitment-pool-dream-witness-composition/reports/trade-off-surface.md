# Trade-Off Surface: Commitment Pool Dream Witness Composition

Fixture: `commitment-pool-dream-witness-composition-public-sample-v0.1`
Template: `templates/trade-off-surface.md`
Review date: 2026-05-17

This surface is deliberation support for the fictional fixture. It does not approve a real commitment pool, authorize real-world routing, establish cultural fit, or decide that the composition is healthy.

## Three Checks

| Check | Fixture State | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Composability | `yes_with_adapter` | Dream, offer, promise, refusal, withdrawal, pool capacity, and witness receipt records can fit through explicit transition records. |
| Coherence | `holds_with_limits` | The fixture preserves capacity arithmetic, contributor authority, refusal boundaries, withdrawal paths, and non-ceremonial witness language. |
| Desirability / health | `promising_for_fixture_only` | The pattern is worth further review because it surfaces pressure points instead of hiding them. It is not yet approved as a real-world operating process. |

## Gains

| Gain | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| Dreams stay dreams unless the contributor authorizes a transition. | Protects possibility from becoming obligation. |
| Pool capacity can return an offer without devaluing it. | Makes scarcity visible without turning a contributor into a ranking score. |
| Post-acceptance withdrawal is modeled. | Treats commitments as living records with repair paths, not irreversible locks. |
| Refusal and protected-source markers survive composition. | Shows that blocked material can shape the route without being disclosed or inferred. |
| Witness receipts remain bounded. | Review records what happened without becoming ceremonial witnessing, legitimacy, or cultural authority. |

## Losses, Costs, Or Burdens

| Cost | Who May Carry It | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The pool frame may make contributors feel pressure to turn dreams into labor. | Contributors, facilitators. | Keep dream records valid even when no commitment follows; make the refusal move visible and normal. |
| Capacity return can feel like rejection. | Contributors whose offers are returned. | Use returned-candidate language and provide repair paths: wait, narrow, or route elsewhere. |
| Steward review can become hidden authority. | Pool stewards, contributors. | Record reviewer scope and contributor authority separately on transitions. |
| Witness language can be over-read as legitimacy. | Facilitators, reviewers, external readers. | Keep the non-ceremonial witness boundary on reports, receipts, and any display surface. |
| More receipt discipline adds overhead. | Jam participants and facilitators. | Use lightweight forms and only deepen review when a transition changes authority, visibility, or obligation. |

## Fragilities

| Fragility | What Could Drift |
| --- | --- |
| `explicit_or_inferred` could be omitted in future transcript-derived records. | Inferred possible commitments could get treated like stated commitments. |
| Contributor consent could be assumed from an old offer. | Pool acceptance could happen without confirmation for the exact pool, time window, and capacity context. |
| Flow-funding edges could add incentives later. | Contributors may feel pressure to keep commitments or make private support routeable. |
| Time windows could expire without review. | Stale commitments could remain visible as current capacity. |
| Display surfaces could simplify the story. | The public story may drop obstruction markers and make the composition look cleaner than it is. |

## Adjacent Possibilities

| Possibility | Required Guardrail |
| --- | --- |
| Add a flow-funding transition after commitment-pool review. | New funding transition, contributor confirmation, and no backfill from refused or private records. |
| Build a participant-facing pool simulator. | Make returned, refused, withdrawn, and pending states first-class game moves. |
| Use AI to surface candidate matches between offers and pools. | AI proposes candidates only; contributors and stewards authorize transitions. |
| Connect to claims / receipt-wall work. | Preserve `derived_from_transitions`, display review, and non-authority receipt boundaries. |

## Foreclosures

| Foreclosure | Why It Is Acceptable In This Fixture |
| --- | --- |
| Protected care notes cannot be summarized, embedded, routed, or used as capacity. | Refusal and privacy are part of the composition boundary, not missing data. |
| Private relational support cannot be backfilled into public commitment records. | Backfilling would change the nature of the gift and create surveillance pressure. |
| The fixture does not produce a public story card. | Display approval is a separate review surface. |
| Capacity pressure does not auto-rank contributors. | A returned offer is not evidence of lower value, trust, or priority. |

## Affected Perspectives

| Perspective | Possible Benefit | Possible Burden | Missing Review Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Contributor making a dream | Can explore possibility without obligation. | May still feel subtle pressure when a pool exists. | Contributor-facing language test. |
| Contributor making an offer | Gets a clear returned-candidate route instead of silence. | May experience capacity return as rejection. | Participant review of return wording. |
| Pool steward | Gets explicit capacity and authority checks. | May be mistaken for the person deciding value. | Steward scope review. |
| Facilitator | Can help record transitions and boundaries. | Could accidentally become the perceived author of commitments. | Facilitation protocol check. |
| Future display reader | Can see what happened and what was blocked. | May over-read receipt as endorsement if shortened. | Display review before any public story. |

## Time Horizons

| Horizon | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Immediate | Did the exact transition preserve contributor authority, refusal, and capacity boundaries? |
| Jam scale | Can participants use this without feeling pushed into commitments too early? |
| Seasonal | Do accepted commitments expire, narrow, or renew cleanly as conditions change? |
| Long horizon | Does the pattern support trust and repair, or does it slowly turn offerings into surveillance and obligation? |

## AI Role

AI can help surface candidate pool matches, likely tensions, missing perspectives, wording risks, and adjacent possibilities. AI should not decide that the composition is desirable, authorize a dream-to-commitment transition, rank contributors, or infer commitments from protected, refused, private, or playful material.
