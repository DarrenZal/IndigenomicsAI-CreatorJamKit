# Discourse: Consent / Review Desk

## Proposal

Keep Consent / Review Desk as an active candidate bundle and required boundary surface, but do not use it as the selected execution bundle.

## Arguments For

- It gives refusal, review routing, attribution, display consent, withdrawal, and tombstoning a visible home.
- It prevents protected or authority-bound material from being absorbed into an executable demo.
- It supports every bundle as a constraint layer.

## Arguments Against

- It can become too heavy for a first public demo if treated as a full workflow.
- Some review routes cannot be simulated responsibly.
- It needs human/steward review before becoming more than a boundary record.

## Evidence

- `docs/jam/composition-v0/contributions/02-protocol-steward-boundaries/`
- `docs/jam/composition-v0/contributions/08-data-steward-consent-withdrawal/`
- `docs/jam/composition-v0/bundles/consent-review-desk/review-required.md`

## Open Questions

- What is the smallest live review queue a facilitator can manage under time pressure?
- Which review states should be shown publicly, and which should stay private?
- How should withdrawal and tombstoning be represented without creating confusion?

## Decision

Active but not selected for execution. Use it to constrain the Open Kit Collective Demo and preserve material that needs review, refusal, protection, or later repair.
