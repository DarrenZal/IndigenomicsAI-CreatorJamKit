# Creator Jam Participant Handout - Offerings, Receipts, and Witnessing

## The Big Idea

The Creator Jam is a place to learn together and build together. People bring offerings: ideas, sketches, prompts, code, practices, commitments, refusals, questions, and relationships. Some offerings will fit together into shared bundles. Some will not fit yet and should be protected or preserved with care.

The goal is not to force one system. The goal is to make contribution, consent, attribution, reciprocity, repair, and next commitments more visible.

## What Is An Offering?

An offering is something you bring into the Jam for others to learn from, build with, respond to, witness, or preserve.

It can be finished or unfinished. It can be practical, reflective, technical, relational, creative, or protective.

Open Kit is a guided public example, not software participants need to install or adopt.

## What Can I Bring?

You can bring:

- a vision, question, or story about what should exist
- a sketch, prompt, note, or design
- a piece of code, prototype, or working demo
- a commitment you want to make visible
- a refusal or boundary about what should not happen
- a source, citation, permission note, or attribution record
- a list of how we would know something worked
- an invitation for others to collaborate

## The Quick Card

Before any template, the facilitator may ask:

1. What are you offering?
2. What do you hope it helps with?
3. What should it not be used for?
4. Is anything private, protected, or review-required?
5. Who should be credited or asked?

## What Is The Offering Integration Session?

This is a facilitated session where offerings are brought into relation with each other.

Together, we ask:

- What fits together?
- What does not fit yet?
- What needs consent or review?
- What should be protected and preserved?
- What could be tried in a small build or demo?

You do not need to fill out technical forms. A facilitator or operator can take notes and translate the conversation into the working materials.

## What Does "Fits" Or "Does Not Fit Yet" Mean?

**Fits** means an offering can safely and usefully connect with a bundle for the stated purpose.

**Does not fit yet** is not a failure. It may mean the offering needs more time, clearer permission, a different bundle, human review, or protection.

Some offerings should remain separate. Some refusals are complete offerings by themselves.

## What Is A Receipt?

A receipt is a plain record of what happened. It is not approval, permission, authority, or proof that something is ready to reuse.

It can record:

- who contributed
- what was contributed
- what permission or review state was named
- what was witnessed
- what was refused, protected, or left for later
- who should be credited or asked

## What Are Build Attempt Instructions?

If a bundle is selected for a small demo, the group may create simple build attempt instructions.

These say:

- what we are trying to build, run, or show
- what inputs are allowed
- how we know it worked
- what must not happen
- what should be recorded afterward

Build attempt instructions help a person or AI tool try something clearly. They do not decide whether an offering is valid or authoritative.

## What Will AI Do?

AI may be used only on public material, or material the right person has clearly approved for that specific AI use.

It may help:

- try a small build or rendering
- summarize public notes
- check whether required fields are present
- show what passed, failed, was partial, was refused, or was not run

AI use should be visible. Failures and refusals are not hidden.

## What Will AI Not Do?

AI will not:

- judge cultural authority
- replace human, steward, community, Nation, or ceremony review
- process protected or private content for checking
- decide consent
- turn a receipt into approval
- make TELUS/Jupyter or any model runtime a requirement for the Jam

## What If Something Is Private, Protected, Cultural, Protocol-Bound, Or Needs Review?

Name that clearly. You do not need to disclose protected material in order to say that review is required.

The facilitator can record:

- review required
- protect and preserve
- do not use
- ask this person, group, steward, or authority
- bring a public summary later, if appropriate

Protected material should stay outside AI tools unless the right authority has clearly approved that specific use.

## What Happens At The Final Witnessing Moment?

The room witnesses what happened:

- which bundle was selected
- what was attempted
- what worked
- what was partial
- what failed
- what was refused
- what was not run
- what remains protected, unresolved, or for later

Only public receipts or receipts that people have explicitly approved for display should be shown.

The point is not to hide gaps. The point is to learn together, build together, remember what happened, and make the next step clearer.
