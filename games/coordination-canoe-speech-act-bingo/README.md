# Speech-Act Bingo

Speech-Act Bingo is a lightweight game for noticing when a conversation tries to turn one kind of record into another.

It can be played during a Creator Jam discussion, review session, spec-writing session, or debrief. The goal is not to interrupt people or police language. The goal is to make transitions visible before they silently become commitments, claims, stories, or funding logic.

## Materials

- One copy of `cards.md` per player or table.
- Pen, sticky notes, or shared doc.
- One facilitator who can pause the room gently.

## Setup

1. Give each player or table a bingo card.
2. Remind everyone: calling a transition is not an accusation.
3. Pick one round length: 10, 20, or 30 minutes.
4. Choose the callout phrase: "transition check".

## How To Play

When someone notices a speech-act transition happening or being proposed, they mark the matching square and say:

> Transition check: I think this may be `<from>` to `<to>`.

The facilitator then asks three questions:

1. Who has authority to authorize that transition?
2. What does the transition not establish?
3. Does this need a receipt, review, refusal, or pause?

If the room agrees the transition was real, the player keeps the square. If the room decides it was not a transition, that is also useful learning.

## Win Condition

The first player or table to mark four squares in a row calls "bingo".

The real win is not the row. The real win is one well-recorded transition that would otherwise have drifted.

## Facilitator Rules

- Do not use the game to pressure people into commitments.
- Do not mark private, protected, cultural, or participant-sensitive content on a public card.
- Do not treat a bingo call as reviewer approval.
- Do not make people explain a refusal.
- Do record useful transition checks as sample notes only if the room approves that use.

## Debrief

Ask:

1. Which transitions were easiest to spot?
2. Which transitions felt risky or uncomfortable?
3. Did any refusal or boundary become clearer?
4. Did any idea become more precise without becoming a commitment?
5. What receipt or template would have helped?

## Promotion To Kit Record

If a transition from the game should become a real fixture or record:

1. Create a `templates/speech-act-transition.md` record.
2. Name the authority check.
3. Name the source and target records.
4. Mark what the transition does not establish.
5. Record an AI-use receipt if any AI touched the source or output.

Game play does not create the record by itself.
